Dr. Muhammad Yunus - A Tribute

Founder of Grameen Bank & Nobel Peace Prize Laureate

Biography

Dr. Muhammad Yunus is a Bangladeshi economist, banker, and social entrepreneur. He is most well-known for pioneering the concept of microcredit and founding Grameen Bank, which offers small loans to the poor without requiring collateral. His work in the microfinance sector has transformed lives worldwide, empowering individuals, especially women, to escape poverty and build sustainable businesses.

Born in 1940 in Chittagong, Bangladesh, Yunus was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 2006 for his efforts to create economic and social development through microfinance. His ideas have influenced policies and practices in financial services across the globe.

Grameen Bank

Founded in 1983, Grameen Bank has helped millions of people access financial resources, lifting them out of poverty and promoting entrepreneurship.

Nobel Peace Prize

Dr. Yunus received the Nobel Peace Prize in 2006 for his work in microcredit and his efforts to promote economic and social development.

Social Business Advocate

He is a strong advocate for the concept of social business, where profits are reinvested to address social issues, creating a balance between economic success and social impact.
